# SafeTrace Governance Charter

## Mission

SafeTrace helps qualified humans transform fragmented records into reviewable accountability work while protecting victims, witnesses, uninvolved people and due process.

## Core commitments

> Empathy for every victim. Evidence for every claim. Due process for every accused. Accountability for every proven crime.

## Authority boundaries

SafeTrace is not a court, police agency, prosecutor, intelligence service or public accusation platform.

SafeTrace does not:

- Determine criminal guilt.
- Conduct covert intrusion or hacking.
- Contact investigation subjects without an authorised partner workflow.
- Publish private addresses or unnecessary personal data.
- Make autonomous referrals to authorities or media.
- Use an algorithmic corruption or criminality score for people.

## Required roles for a controlled pilot

| Role | Responsibility | Cannot approve alone |
|---|---|---|
| Partner case owner | Defines case purpose, lawful scope and escalation | Security exceptions, sensitive public claims |
| Investigator | Reviews sources, entities and chronology | Their own sensitive publication |
| Editorial reviewer | Checks wording, evidence state and contradictions | Legal sufficiency where legal review is required |
| Legal/privacy reviewer | Reviews legal basis, privacy, publication and reply duties | Technical security acceptance |
| Security administrator | Operates access, logging and incident controls | Case findings or publication |
| Auditor | Reviews logs, decisions and control evidence | Operational changes they are auditing |
| SafeTrace maintainer | Maintains software, schemas and documented methods | External assurance or partner authority |

## Separation of duties

- The person who proposes a sensitive claim should not be its only approver.
- Access administration is separate from case-content approval.
- Legal and security gates cannot be self-certified by the project maintainer.
- Corrections and incident invalidations require a traceable decision.

## Conflicts of interest

Contributors and reviewers disclose interests that could reasonably affect a case, including financial ties, political roles, employment, family relationships, advocacy leadership and prior involvement.

A disclosed interest does not automatically disqualify participation. The partner records whether the person is recused, limited to a technical role or permitted to continue with transparency.

## Publication

Every material public claim must have:

- Exact wording.
- Evidence state.
- Supporting sources.
- Contradicting or limiting evidence.
- Human reviewer and rationale.
- Legal review and right-of-reply state where required.
- Correction route.

## Corrections

SafeTrace corrections are dated and linked to the affected claim. The correction records:

- What changed.
- Why it changed.
- Which sources or review decisions caused the change.
- Whether public pages, partner exports or authority referrals were affected.

Material errors are not silently overwritten.

## Partner governance

Before a v1.0 pilot, SafeTrace and the partner sign or formally approve:

- Purpose and case type.
- Roles and controller/processor responsibilities.
- Data categories and prohibited data.
- Access, retention and deletion rules.
- Publication and right-of-reply rules.
- Incident and escalation contacts.
- Metrics, stop conditions and pilot end date.

## Stop authority

Any authorised reviewer may pause publication or sensitive processing when they identify credible risk to a person, evidence integrity, legal compliance or system security. Restart requires the responsible partner role and relevant security/legal approval.
