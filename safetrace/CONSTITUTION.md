# SafeTrace Investigation Constitution

SafeTrace is public-interest investigation infrastructure. Its purpose is to make fragmented evidence understandable without replacing courts, journalists, investigators, affected communities or democratic institutions.

> Empathy for every victim. Evidence for every claim. Due process for every accused. Accountability for every proven crime.

## 1. Mission

SafeTrace helps qualified humans collect, preserve, connect, review and explain lawfully available information. It may reduce repetitive work and expose accountability gaps. It must never manufacture certainty, guilt or public outrage.

## 2. Human responsibility

AI may discover, sort, extract, compare, suggest and explain. Humans remain responsible for:

- accepting a case;
- confirming identities and relationships;
- classifying consequential claims;
- assessing public interest and proportionality;
- legal and editorial review;
- publication, correction and referral decisions.

No model output is evidence merely because it is fluent or confident.

## 3. Evidence before accusation

Every material public claim requires:

- an explicit evidence status;
- a source and precise source anchor;
- a reproducible provenance trail;
- visible limitations and uncertainty;
- consideration of contradictory evidence;
- a recorded human review decision.

Facts, court findings, official allegations, analytical red flags and unresolved gaps must remain separate.

## 4. Due process and fairness

SafeTrace applies the same standards regardless of party, ideology, nationality, institution or social status. The system must:

- seek alternative explanations;
- preserve exculpatory and contradicting material;
- avoid guilt by association;
- avoid treating temporal proximity as causation;
- distinguish lawful influence, advocacy and profit from corruption;
- provide right-of-reply and correction paths where appropriate.

## 5. Victim and witness protection

The safety and dignity of victims, witnesses and vulnerable people take priority over speed, attention and publication completeness.

SafeTrace does not publicly expose private addresses, unnecessary personal data, health information, children, protected witnesses or tactically sensitive conflict information.

## 6. Lawful methods only

SafeTrace does not support:

- hacking, credential testing or unauthorised access;
- impersonation, covert contact or social engineering;
- harassment, doxxing or vigilantism;
- facial identification of private individuals;
- automated suspect lists or guilt scores;
- autonomous referrals to police, prosecutors or media.

## 7. Data minimisation

Collect only what is necessary for a defined public-interest question. Every case requires:

- a stated purpose;
- defined data classes;
- a lawful basis where personal data is involved;
- retention and deletion rules;
- access roles;
- an escalation route for incidents and complaints.

## 8. Security as a release condition

Sensitive-data features are not enabled because a developer completed the code. They require production authentication, encryption, secrets management, access review, backups, incident response, independent assessment and partner-specific approval.

The system must fail closed when controls are missing.

## 9. Transparency and corrections

SafeTrace publishes its methodology, evidence labels, significant limitations and correction history. Corrections must not be silently overwritten.

## 10. Independence and conflicts

Contributors disclose relevant financial, political, professional and personal conflicts. Funding or partnerships may not control evidentiary conclusions.

## 11. Measured impact

SafeTrace distinguishes:

- **outputs** — records, timelines, graphs, dashboards and case packs;
- **outcomes** — saved time, corrected records, answered questions and improved review;
- **impact** — reduced harm, stronger oversight and better institutional practice.

The project must not claim societal impact based only on page views, model outputs or synthetic tests.

## 12. Stop-work authority

Any team member may pause collection, analysis or publication when they identify a credible safety, privacy, legal, evidentiary or conflict-of-interest risk. Resuming requires a documented decision by the responsible human lead.

## 13. Version principle

A version number describes implemented and tested capability. It does not grant permission to handle new categories of data or make stronger public claims.
