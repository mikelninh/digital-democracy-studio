# SafeTrace Methodology

## Purpose

SafeTrace helps people understand fragmented public-interest evidence while preserving uncertainty, due process and human accountability.

It is not a court, law-enforcement agency, intelligence service or accusation platform.

## Evidence states

Every public statement must be assigned one of these states:

1. **Verified fact** — directly supported by a primary official record or independently verifiable source.
2. **Court-established** — established by a judgment, with jurisdiction, date, appeal and finality status shown.
3. **Official allegation** — stated in an official investigation, charge or proceeding but not finally established.
4. **Analytical red flag** — a documented inconsistency, pattern or relationship that warrants examination but proves nothing by itself.
5. **Unresolved gap** — information expected for meaningful accountability that is absent, fragmented, inaccessible or withheld.
6. **Corrected or disproved** — a previous statement or inference has been corrected, contradicted or invalidated.

## Source hierarchy

Sources are not treated as equivalent. SafeTrace records source type and limitations.

A typical hierarchy is:

- Final court judgment or authoritative legal record.
- Official register, parliamentary record, audit or regulator publication.
- Official investigation, charge or institutional statement.
- Primary company or organisation disclosure.
- Reputable investigative reporting with visible sourcing.
- Academic or civil-society analysis.
- Public tip, anonymous statement or unverified online material.

A lower-ranked source can still be valuable, but its status and corroboration requirements must remain visible.

## Relationship rules

A relationship edge is created only when there is a documented basis, such as:

- Official identifier or register number.
- Disclosed ownership or leadership role.
- Published donation or payment record.
- Recorded meeting or lobbying entry.
- Contract, procurement or export record.
- Court, audit or enforcement document.

Similarity of names, photographs, writing style or timing alone is not sufficient.

## Causation rule

Sequence and association do not establish causation.

SafeTrace may say:

> This donation was disclosed on this date. The donor declared these policy interests. These documented meetings and decisions followed. No causal conclusion has been established.

SafeTrace may not say a decision was purchased unless reliable evidence establishes bribery, unlawful influence or another relevant offence.

## Human review

AI may assist with:

- Extracting entities, dates, amounts and citations.
- Normalising records.
- Suggesting possible duplicate entities.
- Summarising long documents.
- Detecting changed official pages.
- Proposing questions and potential contradictions.

AI may not independently:

- Determine guilt.
- Publish a sensitive claim.
- Resolve an ambiguous identity match.
- Contact an investigation subject.
- Refer a person to authorities.
- Remove uncertainty labels.

## Corrections

Corrections are public, dated and linked to the affected claim. Material corrections explain:

- What was wrong or incomplete.
- Why it happened.
- Which sources changed the assessment.
- Who approved the correction.
- Whether downstream exports or referrals were affected.

## Privacy and victim protection

SafeTrace minimises personal data and does not republish private addresses merely because an official record contains them. Victim identities and sensitive evidence require stronger access controls than public institutional data.

Real victim intake remains out of scope until secure storage, consent, retention, deletion, incident response and qualified legal review are implemented.

## Publication test

Before publication, reviewers ask:

1. What exactly is the claim?
2. Which evidence directly supports it?
3. What evidence contradicts or limits it?
4. Is the person or organisation correctly identified?
5. Is the language proportionate to the evidence state?
6. Is publication necessary and in the public interest?
7. Does it expose a victim, witness or uninvolved private person?
8. Is a right of reply required?
9. Can a reader reproduce the conclusion?
10. Is the correction route visible?

## Core principle

> Brave enough to investigate. Disciplined enough not to prejudge. Compassionate enough never to forget the victims.
